Welcome to our final project at Bar-Ilan University, the culmination of our academic journey. We have been working on this project as part of our academic degree in the Department of Information Sciences at Bar-Ilan University.

The Problem of Deepfakes and the Team's Objective

Deepfakes, or realistic synthetic media generated using machine learning algorithms, have become a major concern in recent years. Deepfakes can be used to spread disinformation or for malicious purposes, and it is often difficult to discern what's real and what's not. Our goal is to tackle this problem by creating an application that can identify whether a picture of a face is real or fake.

Our application

We trained machine-learning models using tens of thousands of fake and real images of faces to achieve this goal. You can utilize this web application to upload an image of a person's face. The models will subsequently deliver a boolean response indicating whether the face is real or artificially created.

To test our detection ability, you can download fake images from the following website: https://thispersondoesnotexist.com/

Conclusion and Appreciation Message

We understand the importance of fighting the spread of deep fake images and aim for our website to serve as a valuable tool in this endeavor. We extend our gratitude for visiting our site and for your support in furthering our mission to uphold truth and accuracy in the digital world.
